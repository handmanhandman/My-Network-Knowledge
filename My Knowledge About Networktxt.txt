🔹 What Is a Network?

If we go and look at the grammar or dictionary, the meaning or definition is:

> "Two or more computers sharing data with each other is a network."



And this definition is not only for the computer world!


---

🔹 Types of Area Networks (Based on Size)

Now that we know what the word "network" means,
it's time to talk about the sizes of networks.

We have different kinds of area networks with different shapes and sizes.
These are usually called area networks.

I want to explain just three of them!
(But actually, there are more than three.)


---

🔸 1. Local Area Network (LAN)

A Local Area Network is a network that covers a limited geographical area like:

A home

A building

An office


When devices in these places are connected to each other and share resources (like printers, files, or internet), we call it a LAN.


---

🔸 2. Metropolitan Area Network (MAN)

As the name suggests, a Metropolitan Area Network is used in cities.

Just like LAN, it uses fiber optic cables, which are very fast for moving data.
These cables often go underground and connect:

Buildings

Colleges

Offices

Other organizations


All within a city or large town.


---

🔸 3. Wide Area Network (WAN)

At the end of our list, we have the Wide Area Network.

This network covers large geographical areas, like:

A country

Or even a continent


The best way to understand WAN is by thinking of the Internet, which is actually a collection of interconnected networks.

But unlike LAN and MAN, a WAN is usually slower than them.
